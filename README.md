# Overview

**OPPA (Open Protein Pocket Atlas)** is an open-source computational biology platform designed to identify and explore similarities between protein binding pockets.

Instead of comparing entire protein sequences or overall structures, OPPA focuses on binding pockets — the functional regions where drugs and ligands interact with proteins. By analyzing pocket-level similarity, OPPA aims to help researchers investigate potential drug repurposing opportunities, explore polypharmacology, and identify possible off-target interactions.

OPPA is an interdisciplinary, long-term research project rather than a funded or time-boxed product effort. The project prioritizes scientific validation and reproducibility over fixed delivery timelines, building outward into infrastructure or tooling only once the underlying science is validated.

---

# Project Status

OPPA is currently in the research and validation phase.

No biological claims, drug repurposing recommendations, or therapeutic hypotheses generated by OPPA should be interpreted as experimentally validated results.

Current efforts are focused on establishing reliable pocket similarity retrieval and benchmarking methodologies.

# Scientific Rationale

Protein binding pockets provide a functional perspective that is often complementary to sequence and whole-structure comparisons. Proteins with low sequence similarity may still contain structurally related binding environments capable of interacting with similar ligands.

Several pocket detection and comparison tools already exist, including fpocket (detection and druggability scoring) and dedicated comparison methods such as ProBiS, KRIPO, and G-LoSA. These tools have demonstrated the value of pocket-level analysis, but large-scale exploration of pocket similarity remains fragmented across datasets, workflows, and interfaces.

OPPA reframes the standard question:

> Instead of asking *does drug X bind protein Y*, OPPA asks: *where else does this binding pocket exist?*

By approaching proteins through their binding environments rather than their overall structures, OPPA aims to support investigations into off-target interactions, drug repurposing opportunities, polypharmacology, and functional relationships between seemingly unrelated proteins.

The project does not seek to replace existing pocket comparison methods. Instead, it aims to provide a unified, extensible framework for large-scale pocket retrieval, exploration, and biological context integration.

---

# Validation Challenges

A central challenge for OPPA is ensuring that pocket similarity scores capture biologically meaningful relationships rather than merely geometric resemblance. Two pockets may appear structurally similar while exhibiting very different ligand preferences; conversely, proteins may bind related compounds through pockets that differ significantly in geometry.

For this reason, validation is treated as a primary research objective rather than a downstream task. The project will evaluate:

* Recovery of known pocket similarities
* Retrieval performance on established benchmark datasets
* Comparison against existing pocket comparison approaches
* Correlation between pocket similarity and known ligand relationships

> The long-term value of OPPA depends on demonstrating that retrieved similarities correspond to meaningful biological or pharmacological signals.

Validation work will be prioritized alongside algorithm development throughout the project lifecycle, not deferred until after the rest of the pipeline is built.

# Key Features

### Pocket-Centric Discovery
Compare the geometry and physicochemical properties of binding cavities across different, seemingly unrelated proteins.

### Proteome-Scale Mapping
Systematic analysis scalable across thousands of protein structures.

### Drug Repurposing Exploration
Identify existing therapeutics that may interact with similar pockets on novel disease targets.

### Off-Target Risk Analysis
Explore unintended protein interactions that may arise from pocket similarity.

### Graph-Based Knowledge Network
Explore complex **Drug → Pocket → Protein → Disease** relationships, once the underlying similarity signal is validated.

### Open Research Infrastructure
Built modularly for extensibility, reproducibility, and community contributions.

# Current Scope

OPPA is currently in the research and prototyping stage. The initial objective is intentionally narrow:

* Detect protein binding pockets
* Generate comparable pocket representations
* Identify structurally similar pockets across proteins
* Evaluate similarity retrieval against known biological relationships

At this stage, OPPA does not attempt to predict binding affinity, docking outcomes, or clinical efficacy. The focus is on building a robust, evidenced pocket similarity framework that can support future drug and disease analyses.

# Core Components

## Pocket Detection
Uses geometry-based detection (Voronoi tessellation, alpha-sphere clustering) via **fpocket** to identify spatial cavities.

Outputs: pocket coordinates, pocket descriptors, pocket rankings.

## Feature Extraction
Each detected pocket is converted into a numerical feature vector: volume, surface area, shape descriptors, hydrophobicity, residue composition.

## Similarity Engine
Compares pocket vectors using cosine similarity, Euclidean distance, and RMSD-based structural comparison to retrieve structurally similar pockets, explore potential cross-protein functional relationships, and generate hypotheses for downstream investigation.

Future directions may include learned pocket embeddings, geometric deep learning, and graph neural networks, subject to demonstrating measurable improvement over validated baseline methods.

## Contextual Mapping
**Drug Mapping** links pockets to known binding drugs to enable drug repurposing exploration and off-target hypothesis generation. **Disease Linking** connects targeted proteins to diseases to map potential therapeutic pathways. Both depend on a validated similarity engine.

## Knowledge Graph
All relationships are stored in a graph database for large-scale exploration and querying, once there are validated relationships worth representing.

# Architecture & Tech Stack

```text
oppa/
├── data-ingestion/      # Fetching and parsing structures
├── pocket-detection/    # fpocket integration
├── feature-extraction/  # Vectorization
├── similarity-engine/   # Pocket comparison
├── graph-builder/       # Neo4j ingestion
├── api/                 # FastAPI backend
├── web/                 # Frontend explorer
├── notebooks/           # Research experiments
└── docs/                # Documentation
```

**Core Computation:** Python, NumPy, SciPy, pandas, BioPython
**Pocket Detection:** fpocket
**Similarity Search:** scikit-learn, FAISS (planned)
**Database:** Neo4j
**API:** FastAPI
**Frontend:** React

# What OPPA Is Not

* A molecular docking engine
* A protein structure prediction system
* A clinical recommendation tool
* A replacement for experimental validation

OPPA is intended as a hypothesis-generation and exploration platform for structural biology research.
